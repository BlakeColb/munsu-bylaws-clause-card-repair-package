## Source Text

7. The Executive Director of Advocacy shall: Academic b. assist members in the preparation and carrying out of academic appeals; pertaining to their academics, to academic appeals, and to university rules and regulations (academic and non-academic); Studies, Executive Committee of Senate, Retroactive Drops and Readmissions Committee, and the University Senate; e. monitor and maintain familiarity with academic structures and regulations of the university; f. liaise with the Board of Directors, the membership, and the University on academic affairs; Financial g. f. assist members in addressing problems relating to student financial assistance, and in the preparation and carrying out of student financial assistance appeals; h. promote and publicize issues of relevance to members pertaining to student financial assistance; Legal legal counsel; j. facilitate the Union’s legal aid service; k. assist students with Residential Tenancies issues they may encounter; advocacy service; Other non-academic appeals and proceedings related to university policies such as but not limited to the Non-Academic Complaints Policy and the Student Code of Conduct; n. work closely with the Executive Director of Campaigns on the development and implementation of campaigns relating to student debt, student rights, academics, student financial assistance, and the reduction and elimination of tuition fees; o. ensure, in collaboration with the Academic Affairs Committee, that all student representative seats on committees or other decision-making or consultative bodies of the University are filled by members of the Union appointed by the Union; p. perform other duties as assigned by the Board or Executive Committee
